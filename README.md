# Interviewer Notes & Experience

- Author: Jorge Feldmann
- AI fair usage: drafted with Claude, edited by the author.

A personal collection of notes, patterns, and lessons learned from years of
interviewing engineers — what I've observed, what works, and what doesn't.

## What's in here

- Guides on evaluating technical skills fairly
- Notes on common communication patterns (including non-native English speakers)
- Frameworks I've developed for structured interviews
- Lessons learned from hundreds of interviews

## Who this is for

Fellow engineers and tech leads who interview candidates and want to do it
better — more fairly, more consistently, and with less bias.

## A note on this content

These are my own observations and opinions, shaped by real experience.
I've been a developer since 1998 and started interviewing candidates
on demand around 2011. A few years later, a manager pulled me aside
and said I had a knack for it — structuring conversations, picking up
on soft skills, guiding candidates, and not being an intimidating
presence in the room. He also asked me to train other developers to
interview well. I've been doing it professionally since 2020.
